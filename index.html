Why Marketing Is A Profit Center This is what happens when a sales guy writes an article about marketing. And why wouldn't I? Marketing is good to me, it's working while I'm selling and also when I'm not. We make a good team. Half of our leads this year will probably come from our marketing efforts, the rest will arrive from a mix of business development efforts and some partner and referral leads. It all starts with a solid Go-To-Market strategy, researched and documented by experts in our market. This foundation establishes the messaging delivered by everyone and everything in our organization from that point forward. But maybe you're not coming from it at that angle. Maybe you're churning on the number of contracts you need signed this fiscal year: Let's say we need to make $10,000,000 in sales. (<--feel free to change this total) And our typical sale is $200,000. That means we need to get 50 contracts signed. As we've said, 50% of those contracts will likely come from our marketing efforts. This is where Ad Spend budgeting comes in, that difficult to pin-down sort of number that has too many zeros. Is there any rhyme or reason behind this number? Yes! Let's walk through it. Half or 25 contracts will come from qualified leads responding to our marketing efforts. This is called an inbound lead. It refers to a new business connection to your company who needs your products and services. About 10% of our qualified leads will sign a contract, meaning that we'll need 250 qualified leads to gain $5MM in revenue. Qualified leads are a subset, let's start with 30% of all leads, meaning that our goal is to gain 250/0.3 or about 830 leads. In the digital space (LinkedIn, Google Ads, our Website, etc), a lead happens when an interested person clicks on an ad or downloads an article, or writes or calls the company in response to something they see or hear. Properly placed ads and articles that make the new connection easy are referred to as implementing a Call-To-Action (CTA). To react to something that represents your company, products, or services, this person has to see it or hear about it. These are called views or impressions of our ad, post, article, podcast, etc. The rate at which people who view an ad and click on it to learn more is typically around 1.5%. We're now at 830/0.015 which is about 55,300 views or impressions that we need to gain these leads. This is referred to as traffic. If this was all ads at a cost per click of $5, our budget would be 830 x 5 or $4,150. (This seems like a low number to generate $5MM in business) Metrics The right way to manage this process is exactly to do that: manage it! To manage Ad spend as well as all of the other components in our marketing plan is best accomplished by collecting and analyzing the data behind our marketing activities.
